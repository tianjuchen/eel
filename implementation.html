Implementation

The variational statement of the problem not only is consistent with thermodynamics, but also provides a generic interface for programming.

Following the theory manual, the implementation can be natually divided into three categories:

  • Constitutive models, i.e. the definitions of the internal energy densities and dissipation densities.

  • Thermodynamic forces, i.e. the thermodynamic conjugate of the generalized velocities.

  • Weak form of the balance laws.

Constitutive models

  • Chemical reactions

    • Fick's first law

    • Viscous mass transport

    • Transport of charged species

  • Electrostatics

    • Polarization

    • Heat generation due to Joule heating

  • Mechanics

    • Deformation gradient

    • Swelling

    • Thermal expansion

    • A Neo-Hookean strain energy density

Thermodynamic forces

Recall that the thermodynamic forces are defined as with constitutive relations (from Coleman-Noll)

In Stingray, the forces are automaticall calculated using the above definitions relying on the derivative material interface.

  • FirstPiolaKirchhoffStress computes the first Piola-Kirchhoff stress .

  • MassSource computes the chemical source .

  • MassFlux computes the chemical flux .

  • ElectricDisplacement computes the electric displacement .
