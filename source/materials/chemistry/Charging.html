Charging

This class computes the chemical energy density and its corresponding thermodynamic forces. We assume the chemical energy density depends on at least the deformation gradient, the concentrations and the gradients of concentrations. This class defines the mass transport of a charged species driven by electric potential.

Overview

The charging energy couples chemical reactions of a charged species to electrostatics. The energy density is defined as

where is the electric conductivity, is the Faraday's constant, is the mass transport viscosity, is the molar volume of the charged species, is the ideal gas constant, and is the temperature.

Example Input File Syntax

[charging]
  type = Charging
  chemical_energy_density = psi_charging
  concentration = c
  electric_potential = Phi
  electric_conductivity = sigma
  faraday_constant = ${F}
  viscosity = eta
  ideal_gas_constant = 8.3145
  temperature = T
  molar_volume = ${Omega}
